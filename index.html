Sun Times Equivalence Thing

Thank you for visiting the website of the Sun Times Equivalence Thing! It was one of my past passion projects. I worked on it occasionally from 2018 to 2020. In 2023 I created and published this website, and I have updated it a few times since then.

The program allows you to input your location and then allows you to choose any day of the year. It will calculate that day’s sunrise and sunset times as well as its duration of daylight, and then find other days in the year that have the same sunrise time, sunset time, or duration of daylight.

The Sun Times Equivalence Thing was last updated April 7, 2024. If you’re interested in older versions, you can check out the archive.


How to use

  • Place name: This can be anything you’d like. If left blank, it will default to your coordinates.
  • Latitude and longitude: Latitude is degrees north or south of the Equator; longitude is degrees east or west of the Prime Meridian. To find your latitude and longitude, look up something like “NYC coordinates” and paste the result into the corresponding boxes. Note that degrees north or east are positive, while degrees west or south are negative. In this example, the latitude would be 40.7, while the longitude would be -74.0. It’s okay to truncate to one decimal place or so.
  • Time zone (winter): This is specified in hours ahead or behind of UTC. Here are some examples in the United States:
    New York:-5
    Chicago:-6
    Denver:-7
    Los Angeles:-8
  • DST rule: American DST rules (“us”), European rules (“eu”), or no DST (“no”).
